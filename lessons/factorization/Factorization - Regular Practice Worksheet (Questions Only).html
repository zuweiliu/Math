Directions: Factor fully unless told otherwise. For SFFT problems show the factored form and list integer pairs. For extreme-value problems show the completed square.

Concept 1 - Factoring by Grouping

1.

Given \(a^2+2b^2+2c^2-2ab-2bc-6c+9=0\), find \(abc\).

2.

Real numbers \(x,y\) satisfy \(x^2+y^2=10x-6y-34\). Find \(x+y\).

3.

Find a factor of \(3^{n+2}-2^{n+3}+3^n-2^{n+1}\).

Concept 2 - Splitting & Adding Terms

4.

Factor \(a^2-4a+3\).

5.

Factor \(x^4+4\) by adding and subtracting terms.

Concept 3 - Simon's Favorite Factoring Trick

6.

Positive integers \(x,y\) satisfy \(xy-2x-3y=6\). Find the sum of all possible values of \(x\).

7.

Positive integers \(x,y\) satisfy \(6xy-10x-21y=49\). Find all solution pairs \((x,y)\).

Concept 4 - Extreme Values by Completing Squares

8.

Find the least value of \(2x^2-4xy+4y^2+8y+11\).

9.

Find the least value of \((x+1)(x+2)(x+3)(x+4)+2019\).

Homework from PDF Scope (Mixed)

10.

How many reduced positive fractions \(\frac{x}{y}\) have the property that increasing numerator and denominator by 1 increases the value by 10%?

11.

Evaluate \(\dfrac{1^2-2^2+3^2-4^2+\cdots+99^2-100^2}{1+2+3+\cdots+100}\).

12.

Given \(x^2+x+1=0\), compute \(x^3+2x^2+2x+2018\).

13.

How many ordered pairs of integers \((x,y)\) satisfy \(x^{70}+y^2=2y\)?

14.

Given positive integers \(x,y\), \(xy-4x-5y=14\). Find the sum of all possible values of \(x\).

15.

For how many integers \(x\) is \(x^4-51x^2+50\) negative?

16.

In an isosceles triangle, side lengths \(a,b\) satisfy \(9a^2-b^2=-13\) and \(3a+b=13\). What is the length of the third side?
