The Big Idea

Geometric probability replaces counting with measuring.

\[ P(\text{event})=\frac{\text{measure of success region}}{\text{measure of whole region}} \]

Two random numbers ⇒ which measure?

Example — P(x + y > 1) unit square

\(x,y\) are chosen uniformly from \([0,1]\). Find \(P(x+y>1)\).

Green = success region above the line \(x+y=1\). Empirical \(P\) → 1/2.
Two random numbers ⇒ the sample space is the unit square, area 1.
The line \(x+y=1\) splits it into two equal triangles.
The upper triangle is the success set, area \(\tfrac12\). \(P=\) 1/2.
🔑 TYPE — two uniform numbers + a linear condition
METHOD: draw the square, draw the boundary line, shade the correct side, divide its area by the square's area.

Homework

HW 1length on a segment

A point is chosen at random on \([0,3]\). Find \(P(\text{point} < 1)\).

Success interval \([0,1]\) has length 1 out of 3. \(P=\) 1/3.
HW 2area · circle in square

A point lands uniformly in a \(2\times2\) square. Find \(P(\text{within 1 of the center})\).

Favorable = unit disk area \(\pi\); square area 4. \(P=\) π/4 ≈ 0.785.
